This consultation would doubtless not have arisen – at least in relation to solicitors – had the SRA not abandoned any interest in structured and targeted CPD. Solicitors used to have a CPD regime which looked like that of their counterparts in many other countries, with evidence required of particular courses taken, maybe even by authorised providers. But the SRA decided that that was too blunt an instrument, which took up too much of their resources to police. And so they threw out the whole thing and replaced it with – nothing. Well, not exactly nothing. There is a requirement that solicitors must provide a proper standard of service, which is different to a rule which requires CPD.
